> A clock-sized object that does one thing: it counts. Twelve seven-segment digits, glowing pale blue, ticking up every token you burn through your AI tools.

I kept paying AI bills I couldn't feel. The spend is real but invisible — it lives in a dashboard you never open until the invoice lands. So I built a thing that puts the number on my desk, where I can't ignore it.

## What it is

An ESP32-powered desk device, roughly the size of a clock, with a 12-digit
7-segment display. It pulls your usage and shows a live running tally — no app to
open, no tab to check. Glance up, see the number, feel the cost. It's the
difference between knowing your AI spend and *seeing* it climb in real time.

## Why it exists

- **Ambient, not interruptive.** It's a number on a shelf, not a notification. The point is to live with the figure until it changes how you work.
- **Honest hardware.** ESP32 inside, real segments out front. Nothing precious — a tool that does its one job.
- **Built for myself first.** This sits on my own desk. I'm putting it here to see who else wants the same thing.
